Summary
My group worked with a startup company to create a digital friend for mothers to reduce emotional and mental health risks during the antenatal and postpartum periods. Through user research, we discovered that mothers don’t practice mindfulness and are more concerned about their baby than themselves. We designed a mobile application concept that can help inform, encourage and empower mothers to have a more positive motherhood experience.

The Challenge

Women’s mental wellbeing and emotions are affected when having a baby, especially during the antenatal and postpartum periods. Our client brief was to provide a digital friend for pregnant women and mothers to reduce the risk of mental health issues during their motherhood experience.

The Research Methods

User Interview
Persona
Comparative Analysis
Prototyping
Usability Testing

The Discovery

We interviewed 6 women who are pregnant or have had a baby in the past year to understand their emotions, concerns and pain points. With the findings, we used affinity diagramming and mapped their experience from prenatal to labour and postnatal periods.

Target Participants
  • Women
  • Pregnant and had a baby in the past year
  • Expats and locals
Interview Questions
  • What are the emotions, concerns and pain points of the pregnancy, postnatal (including breastfeeding) journey?
  • How do mothers practice mindfulness through this journey?
  • How can we develop the app to support mothers to practice mindfulness and what features they would find useful?
Mothers are more concerned for their baby than themselves.
They research heavily for their baby and not themselves.
They don’t practice mindfulness. 

We were overwhelmed by the vast findings of the prenatal, labour and postnatal experiences. Due to the limited time, we decided to prioritise on the prenatal experience and consider the other stages as the next steps.

The Definition

Following our findings, we created a persona to help us focus on the pregnant woman’s ideal goals and frustrations.
Problem Statement
  • Lauren needs a way to have a more positive motherhood experience but lacks the understanding of how taking care of her personal well-being can help.

Key Questions
  • How might we inform Lauren on the importance of taking care of herself during and after pregnancy?
  • How might we encourage Lauren through the pre and postnatal journey?
  • How might we empower Lauren to have a more positive motherhood experience?

The Development

We conducted a comparative analysis to understand the solutions available in the market that Lauren would ideally need. We compared with mobile apps on mindfulness, wellness tracking and prenatal/postnatal information.
Tailored content to inform

The app encourages Lauren to key in her moods and symptoms. With these, content is customised to guide her through her challenges in a more positive manner. If she chooses not to key in, she will be recommended content based on her stage of pregnancy. 

These include articles, videos, podcasts, and quotes. Lauren can search for wellness topics using the Explore tab or search bar. She can bookmark and share the content that she has accessed.

Encourage through journaling

Lauren can also log her thoughts, and upload photos into the app to remind her of the beautiful moments. These inputs will be collated into a journal and a gallery. These can also be put together in a digital or print album.

Encourage with stats

The Stats screen is a log of Lauren’s moods, priorities and accessed content. She can earn badges to motivate her to read, watch and listen. She can customise and download the information she wants into a pdf journal. 

Empower through helpline

Lauren is able to reach out to healthcare professionals when she needs to cope with issues like depression, anxiety and breastfeeding difficulties. 

Tests and iterations

We conducted a usability test on 5 participants with the 1st iteration to gain insights into the app’s usability, their impression of the look and feel and the feedback on features and information. We applied Semantic Differential Scale (SDS) for further quantitative analysis. 

As the 1st test findings were vast, we prioritised the issues through affinity diagramming. With the prioritised findings, we made improvements to the 2nd prototype iteration. 

Key Issues – Input
  • Some mood descriptors are similar.
Iteration
  • Streamlined the mood descriptors.
Key Issues – Home Screen
  • Unsure if Today’s Read is the focus article as it has a bigger photo. 
Iteration
  • Streamlined the mood descriptors.
Key Issues – Journal
  • Unsure where their inputs went.
  • Want to click mood chart date to see that day’s entry.
  • Cards didn’t appear to be clickable.
  • Unsure what Export Timeline is. 
Iteration
  • Revised the navigation of the Journal, Gallery and Insights features to streamline them. And renamed the sections Thoughts, Snaps and Stats.
  • Combined 2 mood screens into 1. Made the points on the chart clickable.
  • Revised the look of the clickable cards. 
  • Added info to Export Timeline button.
Key Issues – Helpline
  • Unsure where to find Helpline information.
  • Would like to connect with other pregnant mothers.
Iteration
  • Added a Connect tab to the navigation bar to access support resources - support groups and healthcare experts.

The Outcomes

Easily informed
  • The onboarding process was simple and keying in journal entries was fairly effortless.
  • The home screen content is sufficient and well organised.
Journaling is still confusing
  • Although information is clear, some participants were confused with the placement of the statistics and the functions of the buttons.
Connect with more to empower
  • Other than seeking professional help, they would also like to reach out to mummy support groups to connect with other pregnant mothers.
Overall Impression
  • The tone throughout the app is friendly.
  • Despite the cool colours, users still feel the warmth (calming, inviting, peaceful, sweet).
