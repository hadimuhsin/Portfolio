The Challenge

Improve the service design of the shoe shopping experience for busy working professionals.

Our challenge was to improve the service design of the shoe shopping experience for busy working professionals. With our research findings, we created The Feeting Room, a self-service in-store shoe shopping experience from browsing and fitting to checkout. Our prototype usability testing proved the potential in this concept, with areas to improve.

The Discovery

We observed and interviewed customers to understand the end-to-end shoe shopping experience.

To understand customers’ behaviours and pain points through their shopping process, we conducted in-store observations at shoe stores such as Charles & Keith, Timberland, Skechers and ALDO between noon and evening time. After that, we conducted interviews on a different set of participants to validate our in-store observations and understand their end-to-end shoe shopping experience, from online to offline. The 5Es framework was applied to organise our findings during affinity diagramming. 

The Discovery

We observed and interviewed customers to understand the end-to-end shoe shopping experience.

To understand customers’ behaviours and pain points through their shopping process, we conducted in-store observations at shoe stores such as Charles & Keith, Timberland, Skechers and ALDO between noon and evening time. After that, we conducted interviews on a different set of participants to validate our in-store observations and understand their end-to-end shoe shopping experience, from online to offline. The 5Es framework was applied to organise our findings during affinity diagramming.

Space Issues
  • Seating areas were crowded with shoppers and non-shoppers alike.
  • Customers had difficulties using the mirrors due to overcrowded conditions.
Information-seeking Issues
  • Customers expect staff to be knowledgeable about the products they stock.
Space Issues
  • Customers had a lot of idle time waiting for staff to come back with their shoes.
  • Staff had to make multiple trips to the stockroom to get sizes for different shoes for the same customer.

We were overwhelmed by the vast findings of the online and offline shoe shopping experience. Due to the limited time, we decided to prioritise on the in-store shopping experience and consider online as the next steps.
