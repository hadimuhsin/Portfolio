The Challenge

Ninja Van’s business goal was to reduce consumers’ and shippers’ reliance on our customer support hotline.

Started from a hackathon, the chatbot concept soon emerged. With its potential to save cost from SMS notifications, the product and tech team needed to develop a seamless chatbot experience for our consumers and shippers.

The Definition

Problem Statement
How might we empower consumers and shippers to be more informed and make shipping inquiries more independently?

NinjaChat acquisition flow.

The Development

Simplifying design and documentation

As chatbot design was new to me, I researched and applied Google’s conversational design framework into our design process. The framework guided us to design and document our user flows consistently. We iterated our conversation tree designs as we needed to find the best way to present to the team and stakeholders so that they can understand easily.

Inquire independently

Shippers and consumers can independently find out about their inquiries on the go. They can speak to a live agent for further assistance. The accessible information would help them have a peace of mind during the shipment process.

Manage shipment on the go

Shippers can also create and manage their shipments.

Be informed about your order status

Consumers will receive timely updates of their parcel statuses. They can also find out more about their statuses directly.

Usability testing

We conducted usability tests on 6 shippers to validate and gain feedback on its features. Although we designed a Botsociety prototype, we decided to use a QA prototype that was already built by the software engineers so that we can understand their experiences more accurately. We applied Single-Ease Questions and System Usability Scale for further quantitative analysis.

Single Ease Question (SEQ) results from usability testing.

Difficult to make inquiries

"FAQs" and connecting to a Live Agent are the most difficult tasks, with at least 1 participant giving up on each task. Participants who gave up are considered as failed tasks.

1st iteration (left), 2nd iteration (right).

Key Issue #1
  • “FAQs” and “Other Issues” are confusing. Most also found the FAQ information unreliable.

1st iteration (left), 2nd iteration (right).

Key Issue #2
  • Long waiting time before connecting to a live agent is uncomfortable. Users would find alternative channels to make inquiries faster.
Key Issue #3
  • Chatbot response needs to be fast to ensure user’s assurance.

The Lessons

Understand users’ needs first

We could have defined our users’ information and inquiry needs better by conducting more research before organising the hackathon.

Test more concepts before developing

Our initial assumptions of the users’ needs from the hackathon could be further validated with more concept testing before committing to the chatbot. Furthermore, we could have tested it in sample sizes in every country to ensure that the product fulfills our users’ needs locally. Discovering our users’ needs at the early stages through concept testing could help us prioritise features to develop.
